When a potential buyer walks into your showroom and asks "Can I actually afford this unit?", what does your salesperson hand them?

In most cases, it is a brochure with a QR code to a generic bank mortgage calculator. The buyer punches in the numbers, sees a monthly installment figure, and leaves — with one number and no real picture of what property ownership actually costs. They go home. They consult their family. They hesitate. They go cold. Your salesperson follows up three times and hears nothing back.

That hesitation is not always a sign that the buyer cannot afford the property. In many cases, it is a sign that they do not trust the number they were given — because they know there is more to the story.

## The Difference Between a Mortgage Calculator and a Property Finance Simulator

A mortgage calculator does one thing: it calculates your estimated monthly loan installment. Enter property price, loan tenure, and interest rate — get one number. That is it.

A property finance simulator does something fundamentally different. It simulates the full financial reality of owning a property in Malaysia — including costs that most buyers do not know about until it is too late to plan for them.

The Property Brief's finance simulator covers:

* **Upfront cash breakdown** — down payment, legal fees, stamp duty, and total cash needed before you get the keys
* **True monthly commitment** — not just the installment, but maintenance fees, sinking fund, insurance, and the actual cost of keeping the property
* **EPF withdrawal eligibility** — whether the buyer can use Account 2 to fund the down payment or reduce their loan principal, and what that does to their monthly commitment
* **Debt Service Ratio (DSR) — two views** — the official bank view (what the bank calculates for loan approval) and the real-life cashflow view (what the buyer actually experiences every month after living costs)
* **Amortization table** — how extra repayments over time reduce the loan faster and save on total interest paid
* **Printable PDF report** — a clean, professionally formatted report the buyer can save, print, share with family, or bring to a bank appointment

This is not a calculator. This is a financial readiness tool — and it is available free at The Property Brief.

## Why This Matters for Malaysian Property Developers

From direct experience running Sales & Marketing at a listed developer in Malaysia, the gap between "what the bank approves" and "what the buyer can actually sustain" is one of the most consistent pain points in the entire sales process.

Buyers are routinely blindsided by costs that were never part of the original conversation. Legal fees. Stamp duty. The furnishing spend that happens immediately after SPA signing. The maintenance fee that starts on VP day. These are not hidden — they are simply never surfaced in a clear, consolidated, personalised way before the buyer commits.

When these costs surface late — after the booking, during the loan process, or worse, after the SPA — they create panic, regret, and loan withdrawal. The developer loses the sale. The buyer loses confidence. The agent loses the commission.

A tool that surfaces all of this upfront does not scare buyers away. It builds trust. And in property sales, trust is what closes deals — not brochures.
