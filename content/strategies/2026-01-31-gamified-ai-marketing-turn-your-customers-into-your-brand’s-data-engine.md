C-suites and GMs: it’s time to rethink how marketing really works.​ AI isn’t just a new channel, it’s a new way people think, search, and decide, and if your plan still relies mainly on Google Search or Meta Ads, you risk becoming invisible in the new customer journey.​

Today, people don’t “search” the old way, they analyze.​ With tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini, they ask detailed questions (not just “best condo KL”), and the brands that show up are the ones with a strong, consistent data footprint.​

The real constraint is capacity.​ Most marketing teams don’t have the bandwidth to produce endless factual, structured content, so instead of pushing the team to work harder, my proposal is to make the whole community contribute.​

That’s the core idea of **Gamified AI Marketing**: turn marketing from a cost center into a movement.​
Imagine customers, agents, and partners earning points/rewards for creating simple content that feeds your AI visibility, short LinkedIn posts, 30-second “why I chose this project” videos, mini-challenges to find useful AI tools, AI-generated visuals via branded prompts, and structured testimonials that help AI summarize sentiment.​ Tie it together with a community leaderboard, and you don’t just run campaigns, you design behavior.​
